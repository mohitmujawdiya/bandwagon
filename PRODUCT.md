# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two overlapping audiences, both reachable in the run-up to and during the FIFA World Cup 2026 (June 11 to July 19, 2026), mostly on mobile, often in group chats and on a second screen:

- **Casual American newcomers.** Around 87M Americans express interest, and 43% are watching specifically because the US is co-hosting. They skew younger, more female, and more diverse than established soccer fans. Their context: a 48-team tournament full of teams and players they do not know, no team to root for, and a quiet fear of looking clueless. The job to be done: "Give me a team to care about and a reason to care, fast, without making me feel dumb."
- **Diaspora fans.** Tens of millions with heritage ties (36M+ of Mexican origin alone, plus large Latino, African, Caribbean, European, and Asian communities). Their context: real pride and identity already attached to a nation. They want to celebrate it, broadcast it, find their people, and pull friends in. The job: "Help me express and share who I am with, and rally my group."

Both arrive via shared links in group chats and social feeds, decide in under a minute, and either share or bounce.

## Product Purpose

Bandwagon is a viral web app that gives a newcomer or diaspora fan a World Cup nation to adopt through a roughly 60-second quiz, then hands them a beautiful, shareable Supporter Passport they are proud to post. The passport becomes their home for the tournament: who to watch each day, what their team needs to advance, friend factions to compete in, and an end-of-tournament recap.

It exists to turn a once-every-four-years cultural wave into belonging: the fastest path from "I do not follow soccer" to "this is MY team, and here is my group." Success means three things: viral spread (share rate and viral coefficient), a captured email and identity list that outlasts the tournament (reusable for the Club World Cup and future events), and daily return through the July knockout peak. It deliberately avoids real-money stakes. The value is identity, story, and community, not wagering.

## Brand Personality

Three words: cinematic, welcoming, audacious.

- **Premium and cinematic.** This should feel like a film poster for your fandom, not a quiz widget. Drama, scale, and craft signal that adopting a team is a big, beautiful moment.
- **Warm and inclusive.** It greets the clueless newcomer and the proud diaspora fan with the same open arms. Never condescending, never gate-keeping. Every nation, from giants to debutants, is treated as hero-worthy.
- **Bold and irreverent.** Confident, opinionated, a little cheeky. It has a point of view and a sense of humor, but it punches up and celebrates rather than mocks.

Voice: vivid, declarative, a touch theatrical, with dry wit. It earns the reveal and makes you feel seen. Emotional goals: anticipation, pride, belonging, delight.

## Anti-references

- **Cheesy BuzzFeed quiz.** No clip-art, no tacky stock photography, no low-rent quiz-site chrome. The result must feel like a designed artifact, not a listicle.
- **Corporate or official FIFA app.** Not sterile, brand-safe, or bureaucratic. The official app is widely disliked; we are its opposite: opinionated, warm, alive.
- **Sportsbook or gambling app.** No neon-on-black odds-board sleaze, no aggressive "bet now" energy. We are identity and story, not wagering.
- **Generic SaaS or AI-startup.** No gradient blobs, no identical icon-card grids, no big-number hero-metric template, no "modern startup" blandness. If it could be any AI app, it has failed.

## Design Principles

1. **The card is the campaign.** Every surface must end in an artifact someone is proud to post. If it is not screenshot-worthy, it is not done.
2. **Make the newcomer an instant insider.** Confer identity and belonging in seconds, with warmth and zero condescension. Explain the unfamiliar without making anyone feel dumb.
3. **Earn the reveal.** Build real anticipation, then pay it off. The emotional arc (suspense, then identity, then pride) is the product, not a feature.
4. **Confident, never corporate; bold, never sleazy.** Opinionated and cheeky, but premium and trustworthy: the opposite of both the sterile official app and the neon sportsbook.
5. **Every nation is hero-worthy.** No losers. The design dignifies underdogs and giants alike, so any result feels like a win worth sharing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast and tap-target sizes. The audience is broad, mainstream, and mobile-first.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`. The suspense and reveal, confetti, count-ups, and any parallax or tilt collapse to a dignified instant state.
- Never rely on color alone to convey meaning. Because nations are identified by flag colors, always pair color with the nation name, crest, and text labels, and keep team-color pairings legible and color-blind-safe.
- Bilingual-friendly. The diaspora audience includes Spanish-first users, so copy and layout should tolerate translated and longer strings, with localization in mind for later.
